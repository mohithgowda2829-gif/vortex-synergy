# Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

- Project name
- Team member names
- Department / college

## Slide 2: Motivation

- Food and medicine waste still happens
- Receivers lack a fair and traceable distribution system
- Medicine safety requires controlled verification

## Slide 3: Problem Statement

- No centralized trusted platform
- Manual coordination causes delay and misuse
- Weak visibility and traceability across actors

## Slide 4: Objectives

- Secure resource sharing
- Fair claim handling
- Safe medicine verification
- Operational transparency

## Slide 5: Proposed Solution

- Flutter mobile app
- Spring Boot REST backend
- PostgreSQL database
- Role-based workflows for donor, receiver, doctor or pharmacist, and admin

## Slide 6: Roles

- Donor
- Receiver
- Doctor or Pharmacist
- Admin

## Slide 7: Architecture

- Flutter frontend
- REST API backend
- PostgreSQL database
- JWT security

## Slide 8: Main Features

- Resource creation
- Medical verification
- Claim and reservation flow
- Delivery assignment and pickup approval
- Notifications, timelines, and reports

## Slide 9: Screens / Demo

- Login
- Donor dashboard
- Receiver browse and claim flow
- Doctor verification screen
- Delivery tracking
- Admin reports

## Slide 10: Business Rules

- Medicine cannot be claimed before approval
- Opened or expired medicines are rejected
- Donor must approve pickup for assigned delivery
- All major actions create audit logs

## Slide 11: Results / Benefits

- Reduces waste
- Improves fairness
- Increases safety
- Gives traceability and accountability

## Slide 12: Limitations and Future Scope

- Placeholder verification
- No push notifications yet
- No advanced live maps
- Future expansion for production use

## Slide 13: Conclusion

- Supports SDG 2 and SDG 3
- Technically complete college-level application
- Can be extended further for real deployments
